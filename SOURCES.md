## Evidence status

The activities are grounded in durable concepts:

- AI systems use inputs and produce outputs.
- Training examples can shape system behavior.
- Missing, duplicated, irrelevant, inaccurate, or private data can create problems.
- A model is only one part of a larger sociotechnical system.
- Human judgment remains necessary when evidence is incomplete or consequences matter.

Named tools, model rankings, product features, pricing, and fast-changing legal summaries are deliberately excluded.

## Project trail adaptations

**My Mini Wrapped** adapts the durable learning pattern from DIY Wrapped: choose a topic, organize a small dataset, visualize it, and add an interpretation. The child-facing version uses no names, files, accounts, Python packages, or external AI service. It emphasizes that visual choices shape a story and that a chart cannot explain causes by itself.

**Seven Tiny Systems Labs** adapts all seven ideas from the WWCode notebooks as compact conceptual investigations:

- A digital twin may diverge from reality when the real system contains a condition the model omitted, such as a clogged pipe.
- A 3D mesh represents a surface with connected vertices and triangles; its detail depends on what is captured and retained.
- A Bell-state experiment can produce uncertain individual outcomes while revealing correlation across repeated measurements.
- Linked blockchain records can make changes to history noticeable, without guaranteeing that the recorded information is true.
- A simplified spiking-neuron model accumulates input, crosses a threshold, produces a spike, and resets.
- Simple local rules can create complex flocking behavior, and a new condition can reorganize the system.
- Computation consumes energy; changing the amount of work can change its resource use.

The original notebooks remain linked for older learners who want a coding extension. Package versions and notebook setup may require modernization.

The wind-turbine example reflects current research uses of digital twins for planning, monitoring, forecasting, and informed maintenance decisions. The resource deliberately describes these systems as tools for reducing risk and noticing possible problems—not as guarantees that failures or disasters will be prevented. Predictive digital twins still depend on model quality, current sensor data, validation, and human decisions.

- [Demonstration of a Standalone, Descriptive, and Predictive Digital Twin of a Floating Offshore Wind Turbine](https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.01093)
- [Digital Twin for Wind Energy: Latest updates from the NorthWind project](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.14646)
