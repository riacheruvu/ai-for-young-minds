# Feedback

Feedback is welcome from learners, educators, families, librarians, and youth-program facilitators.

## Privacy first

An adult should submit public feedback on behalf of a child.

Do not include:

- A child’s name or account name
- Exact age or birth date
- School, club, or precise location
- Email, phone number, or other contact information
- Health, family, or private conversation details
- Photos, recordings, or identifying work samples

## Useful feedback

The most useful reports describe:

- Which activity was used
- What made a learner curious
- What claim or answer a learner revised
- Where instructions became confusing
- Where interest dropped
- What an adult needed in order to facilitate
- One specific improvement or next experiment

The website’s feedback builder prepares a de-identified note locally. It does not transmit or store the response. An adult can copy that note into the repository’s learner or educator feedback issue form.
